# MCAT Prep/Tracker Website Essentials

Below are the key features and sections you should include in your MCAT prep dashboard.

---

## 1. Main Dashboard/Home

Your main dashboard should show a quick overview of your progress.

**Include:**

- Days until test date
- Today's study tasks
- Weekly study hours
- Current average practice score
- Recent practice test scores
- Weakest subjects/topics
- Anki or flashcard reviews due
- Progress toward weekly goals
- Study streak or consistency tracker

**Main purpose:**

> Answer: "What should I do today, and how am I doing overall?"

---

## 2. Study Schedule/Calendar

A calendar helps you organize your prep plan.

**Include:**

- Test date countdown
- Daily/weekly study schedule
- Study blocks
- Subject-specific scheduling:
  - Biology/Biochemistry
  - Chemistry/Physics
  - Psychology/Sociology
  - CARS
- Rest days
- Practice test dates
- Content review deadlines
- Automatic rescheduling for missed tasks
- Weekly goal setting

**Nice feature:**

- Color-code each MCAT section

---

## 3. Content Review Tracker

Track what topics you have covered and what still needs work.

### MCAT Sections

**Chemical and Physical Foundations**

- General Chemistry
- Organic Chemistry
- Physics
- Biochemistry

**Critical Analysis and Reasoning Skills**

- Passage practice
- Timing
- Question type review

**Biological and Biochemical Foundations**

- Biology
- Biochemistry
- Experimental design

**Psychological, Social, and Biological Foundations**

- Psychology
- Sociology
- Research methods/statistics

### Status Options

- Not started
- In progress
- Reviewed
- Needs practice
- Mastered

---

## 4. Practice Question Tracker

This is one of the most important parts of the website.

**Track:**

- Date
- Resource used
- Section
- Topic
- Number correct
- Number attempted
- Percentage correct
- Timed or untimed
- Difficulty
- Notes

**Example Table:**

| Date | Section | Topic | Correct | Total | Percent | Timed? |
|---|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| June 16 | Bio/Biochem | Enzymes | 18 | 25 | 72% | Yes |

**Useful Graphs:**

- Accuracy over time
- Accuracy by topic
- Accuracy by MCAT section
- Timed vs. untimed performance

---

## 5. Mistake/Error Log

An error log helps you learn from missed questions.

**Track:**

- Question source
- Section
- Topic/subtopic
- Why you missed it
- Correct answer explanation
- Your takeaway
- Whether it needs review
- Retest date

**Mistake Categories:**

- Content gap
- Misread question
- Misread passage
- Calculation error
- Timing issue
- Wrong reasoning
- Fell for trap answer
- Did not know formula
- Guessed

**Main purpose:**

> Find patterns in your mistakes instead of only looking at scores.

---

## 6. Full-Length Exam Tracker

Create a dedicated section for full-length practice exams.

**Track:**

- Date taken
- Exam source
- Total score
- Section scores
- Percentiles, if available
- Time taken
- Testing conditions:
  - Timed
  - One sitting
  - With breaks
  - Real test conditions

**Example Table:**

| Exam | Date | Total | C/P | CARS | B/B | P/S |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| FL 1 | June 16 | 505 | 126 | 125 | 127 | 127 |

**Include:**

- Total score trend graph
- Section score trend graph
- Review status for each exam
- Biggest lessons from each exam

---

## 7. Analytics and Progress Reports

Analytics help you understand where you are improving and where you are weak.

**Useful Analytics:**

- Accuracy by subject
- Accuracy by topic
- Accuracy by question type
- Timed vs. untimed performance
- Practice volume per week
- Hours studied per week
- Full-length score trend
- Weakness ranking
- Most common mistake types
- Predicted score range
- Improvement over time

**Example Metrics:**

- Biology accuracy: 78%
- Biochemistry accuracy: 65%
- CARS timing: Needs work
- Physics equations: Frequent misses
- Psychology/Sociology terms: Improving

---

## 8. CARS Practice Tracker

CARS is different from the science sections, so it should have its own tracker.

**Track:**

- Number of passages completed
- Accuracy per passage
- Time per passage
- Passage difficulty
- Notes on reasoning mistakes

**Question Types:**

- Main idea
- Author's tone
- Inference
- Function
- Detail
- New information/application

**Useful CARS Metrics:**

- Average minutes per passage
- Accuracy by passage type
- Accuracy by question type
- Timing consistency

---

## 9. Flashcard/Spaced Repetition Tracker

If you use Anki or your own flashcards, track your review progress.

**Track:**

- Cards due today
- Cards reviewed
- New cards added
- Mature cards
- Missed cards
- Retention rate
- Topics with low retention

**If You Do Not Build Flashcards:**

You can still include a section for:

- Review tasks
- Terms to memorize
- Formulas to memorize
- Topics that need repeated review

---

- AAMC Section Bank
- UWorld
- Kaplan
- Princeton Review
- Khan Academy
- Jack Westin
- Anki decks
- Blueprint exams
- Altius exams

**Track:**

- Resource type
- Completion percentage
- Total questions
- Questions completed
- Accuracy
- Notes

A built-in formula reference can be useful for quick review.

**Include Formulas For:**

- Physics
- General Chemistry
- Equilibrium
- Electrochemistry
- Fluids
- Circuits
- Thermodynamics
- Kinematics
- Optics
- Acid/base chemistry

**Useful Features:**

- Practice recall button

---

## 12. High-Yield Notes Section

Create a place for your personal notes.

**Features:**

- Searchable notes
- Tags by subject/topic
- Markdown support
- Link notes to missed questions
- Mark notes as "needs review"
- Add diagrams/images

**Note Categories:**

- Content notes
- Mistake summaries
- Formula notes
- CARS strategies
- Full-length review notes
- Test-day strategies

---

## 13. Goals and Milestones

Goals help keep your studying structured and motivating.

**Track:**

- Target score
- Current score
- Target test date
- Weekly study hour goal
- Daily question goal
- Number of full-lengths completed
- Practice questions completed
- Content review completion percentage

**Milestone Examples:**

- Finish content review
- Complete first full-length
- Reach 510
- Complete AAMC Section Bank
- Finish 1,000 practice questions
- Review all missed questions

---

## 14. Daily Study Log

A daily log helps track consistency and reflection.

**Track:**

- Date
- Hours studied
- Tasks completed
- Questions completed
- Accuracy
- Subject studied
- Energy level
- Confidence level
- Notes/reflection

**Reflection Prompts:**

- What did I learn today?
- What confused me?
- What should I review tomorrow?
- What mistake pattern showed up?

---

## 15. Test-Day Readiness Checklist

Near the exam, include a checklist for final preparation.

**Checklist Items:**

- [ ] Test location confirmed
- [ ] Valid ID ready
- [ ] AAMC login ready
- [ ] Snacks packed
- [ ] Break plan made
- [ ] Sleep schedule adjusted
- [ ] Last full-length completed
- [ ] Formulas reviewed
- [ ] Weak topics reviewed
- [ ] No heavy studying day before

---

## 16. Notifications and Reminders

Reminders can help you stay consistent.

**Reminder Types:**

- Study session reminders
- Flashcard due reminders
- Full-length scheduled reminders
- Missed question review reminders
- Weekly progress report
- Rest day reminders
- Test date countdown alerts

**Build First:**

1. Dashboard
2. Study schedule/calendar
3. Practice question tracker
4. Error log
5. Full-length exam tracker
6. Analytics by topic and section
7. Content review tracker

---

## Minimum Data to Track

At minimum, every study or practice entry should include:

- Date
- Section
- Topic
- Resource
- Questions attempted
- Questions correct
- Time spent
- Mistake type
- Notes
- Review needed?

---

## Key Questions Your Dashboard Should Answer

Your MCAT prep dashboard should help answer:

1. What do I need to study today?
2. What am I weak in?
3. Am I improving?
4. Am I on track for my goal score?
